Garden Defender

Genre: Tower Defense
Plataforma: PC
Developers: solo
Development time: Less than 6 months

Garden Defender was a tower defense developed on my vacation. It was made to understand how the tower defense system worked, in a practical way, since I still didn't have much experience in programming in this style more focused on level design. In it I had to deal with ways to fix art problems right in the code and modularize important information for a level designer. In general, programming the game was even easy, given the short production time.

It was a lot of fun doing the illustrations and character concepts for the game, which was very based on plant-versus-zombies. There were several perspective tricks to make the turrets read well with this type of top-down camera. All the textures of the scenarios and the towers were hand-painted.

In terms of content and mechanics, it's pretty simple, in part because it's a remake of an old game made in Game Maker, and it was an interesting challenge trying to make it fun with what little I had. It still has some problems, but it served its purpose well.
